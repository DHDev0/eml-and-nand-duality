# The EML–NAND Duality: A Mathematical Bridge Between Continuous and Discrete Universality

**Abstract.** We establish a rigorous mathematical correspondence between two universal computational primitives: the EML operator $\operatorname{eml}(x,y) = e^x - \ln y$ of Odrzywołek [1], which generates all elementary functions via the grammar $S \to 1 \mid \operatorname{eml}(S,S)$, and the NAND gate, which generates all Boolean functions. Our main contribution is a constructive proof of equivalence via four explicit transitions: (1) an exact algebraic identity expressing soft NAND $1-ab$ as an EML finite binary tree; (2) an $\varepsilon$-relaxation of NAND to continuous gates $G_{\varepsilon}$ with bounded noise; (3) a circuit construction implementing $\operatorname{eml}$ via $\varepsilon$-NAND gates with Taylor truncation and signal restoration; and (4) a limiting argument recovering exact EML from its approximations. Central to the reverse direction is a signal restoration theorem showing that the iterated double-NAND $R(x) = \operatorname{NAND}(\operatorname{NAND}(x,x), \operatorname{NAND}(x,x))$ with ideal dynamics $T(x) = 2x^2 - x^4$ contracts encoding errors from $\delta$ to $4\delta^2 + 4\varepsilon$, yielding a fixed-point precision of $\Theta(\varepsilon)$. We prove that the real algebra generated by EML-computable functions is dense in $C([0,1]^n, \mathbb{R})$ by Stone–Weierstrass and that the uniform closure of EML-computable functions equals $C([0,1]^n, [0,1])$; we establish that the NAND-algebra $\mathcal{N}$ is not dense in $C([0,1]^n,[0,1])$ due to the Boolean-at-corners invariant, with $\overline{\mathcal{N}} \subseteq C_{B}([0,1]^n,[0,1])$ (the continuous functions Boolean at the cube's vertices); and we derive the Duality Theorem establishing the equivalence of the uniform closure of EML-computable functions and $\varepsilon$-NAND computability with restoration.
